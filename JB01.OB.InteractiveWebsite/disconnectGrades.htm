YOU ARE NOT YOUR GRADES

I'll say it again, you are not your grades.

When you grow up being told you are a gifted student destined for great things, it's very easy to have your grades be make gives you your purpose in life.

In an ideal situation, someone might get a D and think something like "dang, should've studied more" or "That sucks". But, when your grades = your self worth, a D might as well be someone telling you "you are worthless."

For me, this was a very real situation. I went from being a straight A student to barely passing classes, and it completely shattered my self worth. I felt like a failure, like I was less of a person because I couldn't meet the expectations that I had for myself.

School changed from a place to learn to a place to survive.

I don't know how much you feel the pressure of doing well in school, or if you do, how that belief came to be. But, I can promise this:

No assignment is worth hurting yourself over. No test is worth telling yourself you're a failure. No project makes you less of a human being.
